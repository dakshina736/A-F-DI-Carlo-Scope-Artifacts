Component 1 — Web Dashboard
Dispatcher & Supervisor facing
Work Order Ingestion
An AWS SES email listener monitors the dispatch inbox continuously. The moment a Maximo work order email arrives from the City of Toronto, the AI parsing engine extracts every relevant field — Work Order ID, activity type, infrastructure, start time, location, and contract — and creates the work order record instantly. Zero manual entry, zero delay.
Work Order List & Detail
Filterable list by status, contract, activity, and date. Detail view shows full event timeline, assigned crew, field photos, route coverage map, and complete audit log.
Deadline Auto-Calculation
VBA spreadsheet business rules encoded into the system once. Call-out time, activation deadline, and MOT deadline calculated automatically from activity + infrastructure type. Live colour-coded countdown timers on every active work order.
Crew & Vehicle Assignment
Dispatcher assigns operators and vehicles from a live availability panel. Assignment pushes instantly to operator's mobile app. Replaces manual Activity Messenger broadcasts entirely.
Vehicle Status Board
Live board — all 80 vehicles showing In Yard, Deployed, Returning, or Out of Service. Departure and return times auto-logged from Geotab geofence events. No more physical gate-watching.
Penalty Risk Calculator
If vehicles haven't departed by projected time, the system shows live estimated penalty exposure in dollars. Dispatcher sees the financial consequence before it happens — not after.
Live Fleet Map
Real-time GPS from Geotab API. GPS tracks drawn as route coverage — completed segments green, pending grey. Bus stop pins update live as operators submit photos from the field.
Maximo Auto-Submission
On work order close, the system compiles the full Maximo record automatically — vehicle, times, hours, salt used, notes. Dispatcher reviews, approves, and it posts via API. No manual web entry.
End of Shift Auto-Report
Shift handover report auto-generated from captured data — active work orders, vehicle statuses, flags, pending items. Incoming dispatcher fully briefed the moment they open the app.

Component 2 — Mobile Application
Field crew & operator facing (Android)
Availability Confirmation
Pre-event push notification to all operators. They confirm Available / Unavailable / Delayed in the app. Live headcount flows to dispatcher dashboard instantly — replaces manual Activity Messenger.
Work Order Notification
When assigned, operator gets a push notification with activity type, report time, activation deadline, and route details. One tap opens the full work order in the app.
Digital Route Map
Assigned route on interactive map. GPS shown live. Completed segments turn green. Replaces paper maps entirely. Dynamic reassignments appear instantly — no phone call needed.
Photo Capture — Bus Stops
Structured before/after photo workflow. GPS and timestamp embedded automatically at capture. Photos trigger CV classification and update the dispatcher's map in real time.
Salt Load Logging
Operator logs each salt load — quantity and location. Feeds into work order record and Maximo submission automatically. Replaces current guesstimates with actual usage data.
Field Notes & Incidents
Route blockages, breakdowns, or condition notes entered in the app appear on dispatcher's dashboard instantly. Breakdowns auto-flag a potential MOT extension to the supervisor.
🤖
Component 3 — AI & Computer Vision
Intelligence layer on top of the data pipeline
AWS SES — Maximo Email Parser
A dedicated AWS SES listener sits on the dispatch inbox. Every incoming Maximo work order email is captured automatically and passed to an LLM-based extraction engine. The AI parses the email content and outputs structured data — Work Order ID, activity type, infrastructure type, target start time, location, and contract number. No human reads or transcribes the email. The parsed record is immediately available in the dashboard for deadline calculation and crew assignment.
Bus Stop CV Verification
Before/after photo pairs auto-classified — Cleared, Partially Cleared, Obstructed, No Change. Result attached to work order as AI-generated verification. Disputes produce an instant evidence package: GPS, timestamp, CV classification.
Dashcam Footage Store
SD cards from 80 vehicles batch-uploaded via a multi-slot station at the yard overnight. Each clip vectorised on ingestion — vehicle ID, timestamp, GPS, scene tags (weather, road condition, time of day), and event flags (braking, obstruction, pedestrian) stored as searchable metadata. Any clip retrieved instantly by vehicle, date, time, or location — no manual scrubbing ever.

Current Process — How It Works Today
Before the platform
STEP 1
Ontario One Call
Staff manually fill a form in the Ontario One Call portal — excavation location, area, depth, cross streets, work type.
STEP 2
Utility Email Responses
Gas, water, electricity, telecom each email back separately with clearance certificates and available dates.
REPLACED
STEP 3
uExcavate App
Third-party app collects utility emails, consolidates certificates, and produces a ready-to-dig date. Being eradicated.
STEP 4
Manual Work Order
Staff manually create a work order with the ready-to-dig date, site details, and crew requirements.
STEP 5
Field Execution
Crews deployed. Photos taken manually. Completion by phone. Packages hand-compiled and submitted to city.

Component 1 — Locate & Permit Management
Replaces uExcavate + manual Ontario One Call process
Work Order Ingestion — Spreadsheet Engine
At the beginning of the year, a spreadsheet containing all planned excavation locations for the season is received. The spreadsheet ingestion engine parses the file automatically — extracting site addresses, cross streets, work types, and contract references. Each row becomes a site record in the system, ready for locate requests and work order creation. Zero manual data entry.
Ontario One Call Integration
Staff submit excavation locate requests directly from the platform — location, area, depth, cross streets entered once and submitted. No separate portal login. Each submission auto-generates a locate record linked to the site.
AWS SES Utility Email Listener
A dedicated email address is configured as the utility response destination. AWS SES listens continuously. The moment any utility — gas, water, electricity, telecom — sends a clearance certificate or date response, it is captured automatically. No human monitors or sorts this inbox.
Certificate Consolidation Engine
Replaces uExcavate entirely. As utility responses arrive, the AI engine parses each certificate, extracts utility name, clearance status, and available dates. Once all expected utilities have responded, it calculates a single consolidated ready-to-dig start and end date — the latest start that still falls within all clearance windows.
Ready-to-Dig Dashboard
Every excavation site shows its locate status in real time — utilities responded, utilities pending, and overall clearance status. A green Ready badge appears only when all clearances are confirmed. Supervisors stop chasing responses manually.
Pay Duty (Police) Scheduling
For intersections requiring paid police duty, the platform tracks the 5-day advance request window. When a work order is created for an intersection site, it auto-flags if the window hasn't been met and prompts the supervisor to initiate the request immediately.

Component 2 — Work Order Management
Web dashboard — office & supervisor facing
Auto Work Order Creation
Once the consolidation engine confirms a ready-to-dig date, a work order is auto-generated with all site details, permit status, locate reference, utility clearance dates, and required crew types pre-populated. No manual creation needed.
Decision Tree Workflow Steps
Each work order follows a workflow based on site type — road repair, sidewalk, arterial, or intersection. Correct sequence auto-generated: inspection → traffic control → saw cutting → excavation → backfill → carpentry (if sidewalk) → resurfacing. Each step must be marked complete before the next unlocks.
Multi-Site Map View
All active worksites plotted on a city map — colour coded by status: Pending Locate, Ready to Dig, In Progress, Completed, Submitted. Operations manager gets a single visual of the entire contract area at any moment.
Crew Assignment & Notification
Crews assigned from the same panel as winter. Each trade — traffic control, excavator, saw cutter, carpenter — assigned as a separate role within the work order. Push notifications sent to each crew member with their specific step, site address, and report time.
Submission Package Builder
When a site is complete, the system auto-assembles the city submission package — permit number, locate reference, all step-completion photos with GPS and timestamps, crew records, and work order details. Supervisor reviews, approves, and submits digitally. Eliminates the manual compilation lag that delays payment.
Weather Integration
Environment Canada forecasts shown against scheduled excavation days. Rain flags give supervisors advance notice to reschedule — avoiding wasted crew mobilisations and protecting open excavations from weather damage.
📱
Component 3 — Mobile Application
Same app as winter — summer-specific workflows added
Work Order & Step View
Crew member opens their assigned work order and sees only their step — e.g., traffic control setup. They see the site on the map, any special instructions, and required photos before they can mark their step complete.
Structured Photo Capture per Step
Each workflow step has defined required photos — e.g., traffic control requires: signage placement, road closure confirmation, site perimeter. App prompts for each in sequence. GPS and timestamp embedded automatically. Step cannot be marked complete until all photos are submitted.
Site Inspection Checklist
Before excavation begins, inspector completes a digital checklist in the app — locate flags visible, permit posted, safety barriers in place. Result logged against the work order with timestamp and GPS.
Material & Equipment Logging
Crew logs materials used at each step — concrete, asphalt, aggregate, pipe sections. Equipment selected from the fleet list. Data populates the city submission package automatically and gives the office accurate cost tracking per site.
Hazard & Unexpected Find Flagging
Crew flags unexpected conditions — unmarked utilities, buried obstructions, structural issues — directly in the app with a photo and note. Flag appears on supervisor's dashboard immediately, creating a documented record before any decision is made.
Step Completion & Trade Handover
When a crew member marks their step complete, the next trade receives a push notification that the site is ready for them. No phone calls. The workflow advances automatically and the supervisor dashboard reflects the update in real time.
